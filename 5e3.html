Research

Recordings were made at different angles, elevations and distances around a dummy head microphone. These recording were made with and without the hear-through audio headset on the dummy head microphone. Binaural room impulse responses (BRIR) were then created. BRIRs represent the impulse repose at a persons ear when they hear a sound from a specific angle and elevation, these were used to analyse the hear-through device in Python. The main aim here was to see if the head-set would alter any of the necessary cues needed to localise sound sources.
